# Course Alignment — mini-pid-theory

## MIT 6.302 — Feedback Systems
- PID design as lead/lag compensator synthesis
- Loop shaping with PID: |S| and |T| constraints
- Anti-windup and integrator management
- Frequency response analysis of PID controllers

## Stanford ENGR105 — Feedback Control
- PID as the dominant industrial controller (>90% of loops)
- Ziegler-Nichols tuning: step response and ultimate sensitivity methods
- Derivative on measurement vs derivative on error
- Setpoint weighting for 2DOF control

## Berkeley ME132 — Dynamic Systems
- PID implementation in discrete time
- Sampling rate selection and aliasing
- Derivative filtering (pole at s = -N/Td)
- Bumpless transfer between manual and auto

## ETH 151-0591 — Control I
- PID theory: P, I, D actions and their effects
- Stability analysis of PID-controlled systems
- Tuning rules: ZN, Cohen-Coon, IMC
- Practical PID implementation issues

## Cambridge 3F2 — Systems & Control
- Classical compensator design: PID as phase-lead/lag
- Root locus interpretation of PID
- Frequency response methods for PID tuning
- Nyquist stability and robustness margins

## Georgia Tech ECE 6550 — Nonlinear
- Anti-windup as a nonlinear compensation technique
- Gain scheduling as nonlinear control
- Adaptive PID for time-varying systems

## Purdue ME 575 — Industrial
- Industrial PID forms (ISA standard)
- Process reaction curve method (open-loop step test)
- Cascade control for improved disturbance rejection
- Feedforward + feedback combined control

## Tsinghua — Automatic Control Theory
- PID controller structure and parameter tuning
- Routh-Hurwitz stability criterion
- Bode diagram analysis for PID design
- Digital PID algorithms (positional vs incremental)
